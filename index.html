THE PRODUCT

PLATE

The plate is made of acrylic and is designed to be placed on a rubber surface, providing a stable and cushioned area for the user to step on. Its hexagonal shape adds both functionality and aesthetic appeal.

RUBBER

The rubber cushion layer absorbs pressure and protects the internal components. It is made from strong, shock-absorbing material that reduces impacts and vibrations, ensuring the longevity and reliability of the product.

PIEZO

The piezoelectric transducers convert mechanical energy into electrical energy when pressure is applied through footsteps. They harness the kinetic energy produced by walking or running, transforming it into usable electricity.

WOODEN BASE

The wooden base is the primary support structure for the device, crafted in a distinct hexagonal shape. It provides strength and stability while enhancing the aesthetic appeal and functionality of the overall design.

SAFETY GUIDE

Step 01

Before operating the device, carefully read and understand all instructions. Make sure the device is positioned on a flat, dry, and stable surface prior to use. When testing the device, wear proper footwear and avoid sharp heels or spikes. Use the device only in dry environments, and never operate it in wet or damp conditions. Do not jump on the device or apply excessive force beyond normal walking pressure. Keep all electrical components away from moisture and heat to prevent damage or hazards. Do not disassemble or alter any internal components unless you are authorized to do so. Children should not tamper with the device’s connections without adult supervision. If any unusual sounds, smells, or sparks are detected, immediately stop operation and inspect the device.

Step 02

It is essential to refrain from modifying the device or any of its components without proper authorization, as unauthorized alterations can compromise the device's safety, functionality, and warranty. The design and structure of the device have been carefully engineered to operate within specific parameters, and any modifications may lead to malfunctions or safety hazards. Additionally, users should avoid applying excessive force beyond what is typical for normal walking pressure. Excessive pressure can damage internal mechanisms, reduce the lifespan of the device, or create unsafe conditions during use.When demonstrating the device to children or individuals who are unfamiliar with its operation, close supervision is necessary at all times. Children, in particular, may not recognize potential risks or understand the importance of correct usage, making it critical for a knowledgeable adult to oversee their interaction with the device. Ensuring that untrained users are guided appropriately helps maintain a safe environment and supports proper understanding and handling of the equipment.
